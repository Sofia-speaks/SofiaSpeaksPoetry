Excerpts from my poetry collection Grace in Transformation

  
  
    

Excerpt from "A Trust Misplaced"

    

      You were different. I welcomed you with open arms into my world, sharing my secrets, my hopes, and my dreams. I thought I had a friend but it was a wolf in sheep’s clothing it would seem. The crazy thing is you felt like my sister. Pride aside I still do miss her. The laugh we shared the conversations that helped me reflect.     

  
  
    

Excerpt from "A Woman's Curse"

    

      I try to live righteous I am still no nun but I like to think I do my best to represent what God would have of me but every now and then I'm tempted by no other than that sly guy that was in the garden with Eve I think it's Adam that has always deceived I see him in different skins always pretending so I'd let him in telling me sweet lies like how I'm so thin.     

  
  
    

Excerpt from "I’d Rather"

    

      Dude, I'd rather masturbate than entertain a date—with a man like you. Because let's be real... you only love you. I'd rather dodge your gaze, 'cause when you look, you don't see beauty—you see a project. "You ever thought of a weave?" "Your hair is kind of thin and see through."
