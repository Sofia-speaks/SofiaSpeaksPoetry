Excerpt from "A Trust Misplaced"

I welcomed you with open arms into my world.
Shared my secrets, my hopes, my dreams.
I thought I had a friend, but you were a wolf in sheep's clothing.
Over time, I felt like the villain every time we spoke.
But it doesn't matter now. I've made peace and put it to rest.
All I could wish you is God bless.

Excerpt from "A Woman's Curse"

I try to live righteous, but every now and then I’m tempted.
At some point, as women we have to realize we are the prize.
Ladies, if I could leave you with one thing:
You are complete and whole. You are enough.
When God made you, He perfected His craft.
Regal, encompassed in dignity.

Excerpt from "I’d Rather"

Dude, I'd rather masturbate than entertain a date—with a man like you.
Because let's be real... you only love you.
I'd rather dodge your gaze, ’cause when you look, you don't see beauty—
You see a project. “You ever thought of a weave?”
“Your hair is kind of thin and see through.”
“You should probably diet too…”
